Work

A few of my favorite projects.

Below are some of my more recent projects. All (except those specified) were written "from the ground up" and focus on clean, organized, and commented code, with clear workflow and version control. Click on the project titles or website links to redirect to their individual deployments. Source code can be found by clicking on the repository links.

PIG Dice Game

A 2 player game in which players roll dice as many times as they wish. Each result gets added to their round score BUT if the player rolls a 1, all his ROUND score gets lost. After that, it's the  next player's turn. The player can choose to 'Hold', which means that his ROUND score gets added to his GLOBAL score.  Default final score is 35 but can be adjusted.

Built with HTML, CSS and JavaScript.

Website, Repository

Fairshare App

Task assigning and budgeting application specifically designed for teams, built with a 4 person web team (and 2 person iOS team) from a pre-existing project/codebase. On this project I specifically worked on UI/styling. I also implemented APIs and basic functionality such as managing state and creating actions via Redux to delete and edit cards.

Built with React, Redux, NodeJS, Auth0 and reactstrap.

Website, Repository

Recirvoir App

A simple place to store your favorite recipes. Search, add, delete, drag to expand tabs, update(double-click) and download a .csv copy of your recipes. Use on desktop for best experience.

Built with React, Express, Node, Draggable and PostgreSQL.

Website, Front-End Repository, Back-End Repository

Smith and Jones Architecture Website

A fully responsive, multi-page marketing website for an architecture company.

Built with HTML, CSS, Javascript, and LESS.

Website, Repository

About

It's nice to meet you, I'm Joseph Chretien-Golden.

I am a full stack web developer, minimal-design lover, book worm, film-maker, video game fan and learning enthusiast.

My exploration into development started with CS 1300, an intro level C++ course at Texas State University. I was far enough along in my studies as a Digital Media major/Business Minor that I decided to finish my degree without switching to Computer Science, but the bug had bitten me.

After graduating from Texas State, having collected some development experience in additional Web Design and JavaScript classes, I worked for Apple as a Mac/iOS technical advisor for a total of 2.5 years until I decided I wanted to take on a developer role. To that end, I spent over a year studying full stack web and computer science, both on my own and as a student/Team Lead at Lambda School. I am now eager to dive head first into my web development career.

If you have any questions about me, my portfolio, or would just love to chat, feel free to reach out! I look forward to speaking with you.
